The New Orleans Saints play in the NFC South Division of the NFL and their home stadium is the Mercedes Benz Superdome (also known as Ceasar’s Superdome) in New Orleans. The team has been named after the popular New Orleans hymn “When the Saints go Marching”. Since their establishment in 1966, the New Orleans Saints did not have any playoff appearances for two decades.

The Saints had their first playoff appearance in 1987 and it wasn’t until the 2000s that the team featured prominently in postseason games. The New Orleans Saints have only featured in 1 Super Bowl game (XLIV) and they won it. New Orleans Saints had a run of 4 consecutive playoff appearances from 2017 to 2020, but the streak ended in 2021. The Saint missed out on a playoff berth in the 2021-22 season even after coming 2nd in the NFC South Division as the San Francisco 49ers overtook them for a spot by defeating the L.A. Rams. The New Orleans Saints missed out on a playoff spot for the second season in a row after they placed 3rd in the NFC South in the 2022 -23 NFL season.
