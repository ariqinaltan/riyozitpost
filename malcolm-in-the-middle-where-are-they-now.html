When “Malcolm in the Middle” first aired in 2000, it quickly became a beloved sitcom for its quirky humor and relatable portrayal of family life. The show followed the gifted yet troubled Malcolm and his eccentric family, including his overbearing mother Lois, dimwitted father Hal, and his brothers Francis, Reese, and Dewey. Frankie Muniz (Malcolm)

Frankie Muniz, who played the titular character Malcolm, has had a diverse career since the show ended. He pursued a career in open-wheel racing and even became a drummer for the band Kingsfoil. In terms of acting, he’s made guest appearances on shows like “Criminal Minds” and “Preacher” and participated in “Dancing with the Stars.” Muniz has also been open about his health challenges, including memory loss and mini-strokes.

Bryan Cranston (Hal)

Bryan Cranston’s career skyrocketed after his role as the lovable Hal. He is best known for his critically acclaimed role as Walter White in “Breaking Bad,” for which he won several Emmys. Cranston has also made a name for himself on Broadway, winning a Tony Award for his portrayal of President Lyndon B. Johnson in “All the Way.” His film career includes roles in “Trumbo” and “Godzilla,” and he continues to be a prominent figure in Hollywood.

Jane Kaczmarek (Lois)

Jane Kaczmarek, who played the matriarch Lois, continued her television career with guest spots on shows like “The Big Bang Theory” and “This Is Us.” She has also performed in theater productions and lent her voice to characters in animated series such as “The Simpsons” and “Phineas and Ferb.”

Christopher Masterson (Francis)

Christopher Masterson, who portrayed the eldest brother Francis, has kept a lower profile. He has taken on roles in various independent films and television shows. Masterson is also a DJ and has worked as a producer on several projects.

Justin Berfield (Reese)

Justin Berfield, known as the troublemaker Reese, transitioned to the other side of the camera. He has worked as a producer on shows like “Sons of Tucson” and is the Chief Creative Officer of Virgin Produced, the film and television development, packaging, and production arm of the British Virgin Group.

Erik Per Sullivan (Dewey)

Erik Per Sullivan, who played the youngest brother Dewey for most of the series, has largely stepped away from acting. He has focused on his education, attending the University of Southern California. Sullivan has made a few acting appearances post-show but remains mostly out of the spotlight.
