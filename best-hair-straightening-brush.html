Can using a straightening brush damage hair?

Harpers Bazaar

According to Lopez, "Anything that is adding heat to the hair is causing some sort of damage." Plus, he notes that "With a straightening brush, you are also adding mechanical damage in addition to heat damage. However, the amount of damage is related to the tool you are using. In the T3 Edge, the bristles were specifically designed to be gentle on the hair to not snag or pull, even you are trying to add bends."

When should you use a flat iron instead of a hair straightener brush?

While hot tools are often subjective, Lopez notes that those with thicker hair may benefit more from hair straightener use over that of a straightening brush. "I would lean towards using an iron on very curly hair that needs a much more sleek polish," explains Lopez. "Straightening brushes are a great way to touch up blowouts on very curly hair, but more so good for instant gratification on wavier hair types when looking for a quick smooth blowout hairstyle."

Can you use a straightening brush on wet hair?

Harpers Bazaar

That's a big no. If you want a tool that styles wet hair, use a blow-dry brush instead, or consider a multitasking tool, like the Dyson Airwrap (which we've tested firsthand).
