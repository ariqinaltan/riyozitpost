Gruesome history of mortuary under Tower Bridge where bodies washed up from the Thames

August 11, 2024
post

Underneath the north side of Tower Bridge, in an area where thousands of people pass everyday, lies a site known as Dead Man’s Hole.

This alcove right by the waters edge looks relatively normal to the unsuspecting passer-by, but it hides a dark and gruesome history.

In the Victorian times this alcove was a mortuary where dead bodies would wash up from the river with an alarming regularity.

Down the eastern side of the bridge visitors will notice an ‘L’ shaped set of stone steps curving down into the Thames, which is thought to have been used to make retrieving the bodies easier.

Once retrieved from the water, bodies would have been left in this mortuary until they could be identified, or until they were taken for burial.

The morbid area does not look significantly uninviting from the shiny white tiles that decorate the alcove, however these too reportedly have a gruesome purpose.

It has been suggested that the purpose of the tiles was to make cleaning up the space easier, as the decomposing bodies brought up from the river could possibly explode due to the trapped gases.

Passersby can now find the area which is clearly signposted as part of the directions for the Tower Bridge exhibition.
