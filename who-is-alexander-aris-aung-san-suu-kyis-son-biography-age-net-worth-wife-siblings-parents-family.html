Biography

Alexander Myint San Aung Aris (born April 12, 1973), known widely as Alexander Aris, is a prominent Burmese-British advocate for human rights, dedicated to championing democracy and fostering human rights in Myanmar. 

As the son of Aung San Suu Kyi, a Nobel Peace Prize laureate, and former State Counsellor of Myanmar, Alexander Aris has devoted his life to advancing this crucial cause.

Early Life & Education

Alexander Aris, a staunch supporter of human rights, was born on April 12, 1973, within the gates of London’s Queen Charlotte’s and Chelsea Hospital. His parents, Aung San Suu Kyi and Michael Aris were responsible for his upbringing and were important in establishing his ideals and ideas. 

Growing up amidst love and guidance, Alexander formed an unbreakable bond with his only sibling, Dannian Kim Arundel Aris. He grew up alongside his grandparents, Aung San, Khin Kyi, Josette Aris, and John Aris, with his aunt Aung San Chit.

Alexander Aris embarked on his educational journey at Magdalen College School and later completed his studies at Northern Illinois University.

Career

In 1989, Alexander Aris and his brother Kim faced the unfortunate circumstance of having their Burmese citizenship revoked by the military junta. Despite this setback, both brothers were fortunate to hold British nationality. Dannian Kim Arundel Aris, just 11 years old at the time, saw his comfortable life in Oxford, England, disrupted when his mother made the difficult decision to return to Burma to care for her ailing mother.

In 1991, Aung San Suu Kyi, the renowned mother of Alexander Aris and Kim Aris, was honored with the prestigious Nobel Peace Prize for her tireless efforts in Burma. Despite their young ages of 18 and 14, respectively, Alexander and Kim graciously accepted the esteemed award on behalf of their mother. The substantial prize money of 1.3 million USD was wisely utilized to establish a trust dedicated to improving healthcare and education opportunities for the citizens of Burma.

Throughout his career, Alexander Aris has received various honors and delivered numerous speeches on behalf of his mother. Notable accolades include winning The International Human Rights Law Group Award, participating in the Olympic Torch Relay in Spain, and receiving the renowned Presidential Medal of Freedom in the United States.
