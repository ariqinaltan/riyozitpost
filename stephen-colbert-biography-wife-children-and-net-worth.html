Stephen Colbert is an American television host, writer, and comedian who uses satirical comedy as a way to please the audience. He is best known for his work on the Comedy Central show, “The Colbert Report,” which aired between 2005 and 2014. Later, in September 2015, he began hosting a new show called ” The Late Show with Stephen Colbert.” Colbert also previously worked as a correspondent on ” The Daily Show,” a CBS talk show. This is the program that gave him great recognition.

Colbert has also gained recognition through various awards throughout his career and was also listed in Time’s 100 Most Influential People in 2006 and also in 2012. It later also became a New York Times bestseller in 2007.

Stephen Colbert: early life, career and nationality

Stephen Colbert was born on May 13, 1964 in Washington DC, which makes his nationality American. He is the youngest among the other 10 siblings of his. He was born and raised in a Catholic family, spending most of his childhood on James Island, South Carolina. Colbert’s father was dean of Saint Louis University, Yale University, and also the Medical University of South Carolina, while his mother was a homemaker.

Colbert’s father and two of his brothers died in a plane crash on September 11, 1974, when he was just 10 years old. Later, he along with his remaining family moved to a more urban setting where he found it difficult to settle down and make friends. In fact, he became more distant at a young age.

Colbert began his career after graduating from Northwestern’s School of Communication in 1986. Along with cast members, Amy Sedaris and Paul Dinell, they created a comedy skit for Comedy Central, called Exit 57. The show became a hit . and lasted for two years until 1997. After which, Colbert joined The Daily Show working as a correspondent. The show gave him wide coverage and eventually he got his own show.” The Colbert Report” in 2005, where he used his satirical humor to comment on political issues.
