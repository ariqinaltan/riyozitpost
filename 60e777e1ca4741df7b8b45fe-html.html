Copa America 2021: Emiliano Martinez: Argentina's trump card in their quest for Copa America glory

August 21, 2024
post

When Argentina step out onto the Maracana pitch for their Copa America final against Brazil on Saturday evening, they will do so with an extra degree of confidence from being able to count on Emiliano Martinez.

It is Martinez who has played the most decisive role in Argentina's campaign so far, as his three penalty saves against Colombia handed Argentina a ticket to Rio de Janeiro for the final.

His performance in the semi-final was the latest indication of how he has established himself as one of the best goalkeepers in world football in just a short space of time.

The Aston Villa goalkeeper came into the tournament having finished third in the Premier League Golden Glove standings, with 15 clean sheets during his first season at Villa Park, and has continued his form throughout the tournament.

Whilst his country have been unable to lift a major trophy since their Copa America triumph in 1993, they have come extremely close in that competition on a number of occasions, specifically during their last two appearances in the final in 2015 and 2016.

Both matches ended in heartbreaking fashion for the Albiceleste, as their two defeats both came against Chile on penalties.

However, Martinez's stunning rise since he was thrust into the Arsenal first team at the end of the 2019/20 season has seen him take the number one spot for his country, before playing a pivotal role this summer.

Argentina's record

Although Argentina have not defeated Brazil in a competitive fixture since June 2005, and will play the final in the stadium where they lost the 2014 World Cup Final against Germany, the presence of Martinez in their goal will ensure that they will not be fazed by the occasion.

He will look to write the next chapter of his own fairytale by playing a decisive role in their quest to end 28 years without a major trophy - especially if it goes to penalties.
