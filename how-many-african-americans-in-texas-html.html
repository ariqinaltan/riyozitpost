Howdy, Partner! How Many African Americans Are Holdin' Down the Lone Star State?

Texas, the land of wide-open spaces, ten-gallon hats, and some of the best barbecue this side of the Mississippi (don't get me started on that Carolina mess). But beneath the yeehaw exterior, there's a rich tapestry of cultures, and a surprising number of folks you might not expect – African Americans! That's right, Texas boasts the largest African American population of any state in the US.

Hold on to your Stetsons, folks, 'cause we're about to wrangle some data!

According to the 2020 census, there are over 3.4 million African Americans movin' and groovin' in Texas. That's a bigger herd than a cattle drive at the state fair! You might be thinkin', "But Texas is all about cowboys and oil rigs, right?" Well, surprise, surprise! African Americans have been shapin' Texas since way back when, from cowboys wranglin' cattle to entrepreneurs buildin' businesses. They've had a big impact on everything from the music scene (think Beyoncé, y'all) to the political landscape.

Where in Texas are these African American movers and shakers?

Well, most of them are hangin' out in the big cities, like:

  • Houston: This metropolis is practically a cultural gumbo with a huge African American community.
  • Dallas-Fort Worth: The DFW metroplex is another hotspot, with a vibrant African American scene.
  • San Antonio: This historic city has a long history of African American culture, and it shows!

Now, here's the kicker: The African American population in Texas is growing faster than the overall population. That means Texas is gettin' even more diverse and dynamic.

So, the next time you think of Texas, don't just picture tumbleweeds and dusty towns. Remember, there's a whole lotta soul food, jazz music, and African American excellence simmerin' just beneath the surface. Texas ain't just big, it's beautiful in all its diverse glory.
